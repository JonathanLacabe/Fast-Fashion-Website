About Us

Art used with permission by Shoaib Pasha

We area factory in North-Western India. We are a family-owned business that has been in business for over a decade. Raghav Merotra is our current employer who keeps 30 employees in work.
Worker morale has always been a very important focus for us at Paldi - we firmly belief in the respect and well treatment of our employees.
We sell a list of items such as jackets, coats, shirts, and caps. We use fabrics such as Wool, cotton, polyester, nylon, and rayon (viscose). We also sell leather, fur, and silk. We are located by the Bandi river in Jaipur, India. We make on average $10,000 per day, with 2750 kilograms of fabric produced per day.
We use a variety of chemicals in our production, many reusable and environmentally non-threatening. We are open for calls and meetings in-person from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, 7 days a week.
Our goal with our factory is to make clothing accessible to all!

Fabrics


Wool is biodegradable. However, sheep farming produces huge
amounts of methane, and cruelty is prevalent.

While biodegradable, Cotton is EXTREMELY water intensive
and is produced using hazardous chemicals.

A synthetic fiber derived from oil, polyester uses less
water, but sheds microplastics with every wash.

Nylon is a non-biodegradable synthetic fiber derived
from oil, producing the greenhouse gas nitrous oxide.

A biodegradable fiber derived from wood pulp, Rayon's
chemical-intensive production endangers workers.

Biodegradable, Fur can be renewable with sustainable
trapping, but is known for cruelty and pollution.

Luxurious, Silk is created from silkworms. It uses lots of
water and utilizes pollutive chemicals in dying/cleaning.

Though long-lasting, Leather undergoes a polluting
dying process, notwithstanding animal cruelty issues.
